Hi! You’re with an ordinary village fisherman who’s seen the indescribable…

Diary entry · August 12

I was checking the nets on Lake Labynkyr. The water was a black mirror, too still. Suddenly it humped up—a dark ridge with a row of fins—then dropped away beneath the surface as if through an open floodgate, like a submarine. No splash. No sound.

Geologists from the Academy listened, but their eyes held no scientific curiosity—only a hunter’s cold arithmetic. A week later, others came—in plain clothes. Their questions weren’t about the creature, but something stranger.

They cared about lights in the sky and a low hum from under the earth. They weren’t hunting a monster—they wanted a pattern, a link between these damned riddles. Of course I was told to keep quiet.

That was when I understood: they already knew. Our lake picked up special marks on their maps. We fish here and think we own the place. Yet down below, in the endless cold, something ancient lies—and it watches. Our silence is the price of a bargain they never explain. They keep us in the dark so we won’t ask: who, exactly, did they bargain with?
